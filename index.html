I am a fifth-year computer science Ph.D. student at Emory University, concentrating on Bioinformatics. It is a pleasure to work with my advisor Hao Wu.

I got my B.S. in Statistics from the Department of Statistics & Actuarial Science at the University of Iowa in May 2016. During my Ph.D. career, I explored a wide range of topics in bioinformatics including: personalized pathway analysis, transcription factor activity inference, scRNA-seq power assessment, and unsupervised feature selections. I focus on statistical modeling and learning in scRNA-seq area. Some of the codes for my research are available on GitHub.
